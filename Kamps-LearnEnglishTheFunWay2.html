📍 Scene 3
Travel Plans — Booking a trip with a friend
Pay attention to future tenses: will, going to, might.

¡Sí! Fui el año pasado. Es absolutamente hermoso. Te encantará. ¿Vas solo?

📍 Dialogue 1
Shopping for clothes — Returns & exchanges
Words underlined in gold are key vocabulary. Read them carefully.
C
Customer
Excuse me, I bought this jacket last week but I'd like to return it. The size is too small.
Disculpe, compré esta chaqueta la semana pasada pero me gustaría devolverla. La talla es demasiado pequeña.
A
Assistant
No problem at all! Do you have your receipt? We can exchange it for a larger size or give you a full refund.
¡Sin problema! ¿Tiene su recibo? Podemos cambiarlo por una talla más grande o hacerle un reembolso completo.
C
Customer
I have it here. I'd rather exchange it if you have a medium in stock. I love the colour and the style!
Lo tengo aquí. Preferiría cambiarlo si tienen una mediana en existencia. ¡Me encanta el color y el estilo!
A
Assistant
Let me check... Yes, we do! Here you go. The fitting rooms are on your right if you want to try it on first.
Déjeme revisar... ¡Sí tenemos! Aquí tiene. Los probadores están a su derecha si quiere probársela primero.
📚 Vocabulary quiz
What does "exchange" mean in this context?
✓ "Exchange" = swap/cambiar por otro artículo. "Refund" is when you get money back.
📚 Comprehension
What does the customer ultimately prefer?
✓ She said "I'd rather exchange it... I love the colour and the style!"
📍 Dialogue 2
Sharing opinions — Social media debate
Notice how each speaker expresses agreement and disagreement politely.
T
Tom
I think social media has a negative impact on teenagers. They spend too much time online instead of being active.
Creo que las redes sociales tienen un impacto negativo en los adolescentes. Pasan demasiado tiempo en línea en lugar de ser activos.
L
Lily
I see your point, but I partly disagree. Social media also helps young people stay connected and learn new things.
Entiendo tu punto, pero estoy parcialmente en desacuerdo. Las redes sociales también ayudan a los jóvenes a mantenerse conectados y aprender cosas nuevas.
T
Tom
That's a fair point. I admit it depends on how you use it. Moderation is key.
Es un punto justo. Admito que depende de cómo lo uses. La moderación es la clave.
📚 Opinion language
Which phrase shows PARTIAL disagreement?
✓ "I see your point, but..." = acknowledges the other person before disagreeing politely.
📍 Dialogue 3
Daily routines — Talking about habits
Focus on frequency adverbs: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never.

¿Raramente cocinas en casa? Yo usualmente preparo mis comidas los domingos para toda la semana.

A veces cocino, pero no tengo mucha confianza en la cocina. Con frecuencia pido a domicilio después del trabajo, ¡es simplemente más fácil!
J
Jamie

Ya nunca hago pedidos de comida. La preparación de comidas me ahorra mucho dinero. ¡Podría enseñarte algunas recetas fáciles!

🎭 Full Role-Play — Restaurant Scene
One person is the waiter, the other is the customer
Try to speak for at least 3–4 minutes. Improvise — make it fun!
🧑‍🍳 Waiter / Waitress
• Greet and seat the customer
• Offer the menu and specials
• Take the order (ask about allergies!)
• Check if everything is OK
• Bring the bill when asked
🍽 Customer
• Ask for recommendations
• Order food and drinks
• Ask about ingredients
• Complain: one dish is wrong!
• Ask for the bill politely
🔑 Useful phrases for the waiter:
"Are you ready to order?" · "I'd recommend the..." · "Would you like anything else?" · "I'm sorry, let me fix that right away."

🔑 Useful phrases for the customer:
"Could I see the menu, please?" · "What do you recommend?" · "I'm allergic to..." · "Excuse me, I ordered the... not the..." · "Could we get the bill, please?"
